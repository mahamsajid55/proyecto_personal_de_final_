✅ Basic Spanish Sentence Structure

Spanish follows a **Subject-Verb-Object (SVO)** word order, like English.

  • English: **I eat apples.**
  • Spanish: **Yo como manzanas.**

✅ Subject Pronouns

These are the words for "I," "you," "he," etc.

EnglishSpanish
IYo
You (informal)Tú
You (formal)Usted
He/SheÉl / Ella
WeNosotros / Nosotras
TheyEllos / Ellas

✅ Nouns & Genders

All nouns are either **masculine** or **feminine**.

  • Masculine: **El libro (The book), El perro (The dog)**
  • Feminine: **La mesa (The table), La casa (The house)**

✅ Adjectives & Their Placement

In Spanish, adjectives **usually come after the noun**.

  • English: **A red car**
  • Spanish: **Un coche rojo**

✅ Common Verbs & Conjugation

Verbs change depending on the subject. Here’s an example with **the verb "hablar" (to speak):**

PronounConjugation
Yo (I)Hablo
Tú (You)Hablas
Él/Ella (He/She)Habla
Nosotros (We)Hablamos
Ellos (They)Hablan

✅ Present, Past & Future Tenses

Learn how to express actions in different tenses.

TenseExample
PresentYo hablo español. (I speak Spanish.)
PastYo hablé español. (I spoke Spanish.)
FutureYo hablaré español. (I will speak Spanish.)

✅ Negation (Making Sentences Negative)

To make a sentence negative, simply place **"no"** before the verb.

  • English: **I don’t speak Spanish.**
  • Spanish: **No hablo español.**

✅ Possessive Adjectives

These words show **ownership**.

EnglishSpanish
MyMi / Mis
YourTu / Tus
His/HerSu / Sus
OurNuestro(a) / Nuestros(as)

✅ Comparatives & Superlatives

  • Comparing: **más + adjective + que** (more ... than)
  • Example: **Este coche es más rápido que el otro.** (This car is faster than the other.)
  • Superlative: **el/la más + adjective** (the most ...)
  • Example: **Este es el libro más interesante.** (This is the most interesting book.)
