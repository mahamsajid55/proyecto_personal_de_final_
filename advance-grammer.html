🌟 Subjunctive Mood (El Subjuntivo)

The **subjunctive mood** is used to express doubt, wishes, emotions, and hypothetical situations.

✅ English vs. Spanish:

  • **English:** "I want you to study."
  • **Spanish:** "Quiero que tú estudies."

📝 How to Form It:

🔄 Imperfect Subjunctive (El Imperfecto del Subjuntivo)

Used for **past hypothetical situations, polite requests, and past wishes.**

✅ English vs. Spanish:

  • **English:** "I wish you were here."
  • **Spanish:** "Ojalá que estuvieras aquí."

📝 How to Form It:

Take the **third-person plural (ellos)** form of the preterite, drop the "-ron," and add these endings:

PronounVerb Ending
Yo-ra (hablara, comiera)
Tú-ras (hablaras, comieras)
Él/Ella-ra (hablara, comiera)
Nosotros-ramos (habláramos, comiéramos)
Ellos/Ellas-ran (hablaran, comieran)

⏳ Future Perfect (El Futuro Perfecto)

Used to express **actions that will have been completed by a certain time in the future.**

✅ English vs. Spanish:

  • **English:** "By next year, I will have finished my studies."
  • **Spanish:** "Para el próximo año, habré terminado mis estudios."

📝 How to Form It:

Use **haber** in the future tense + past participle.

PronounFuture Form of HaberPast Participle
Yohabréterminado
Túhabrásterminado
Él/Ellahabráterminado
Nosotroshabremosterminado
Ellos/Ellashabránterminado

📢 Passive Voice (La Voz Pasiva)

Used to emphasize the **action rather than the subject performing it.**

✅ English vs. Spanish:

  • **English:** "The book was written by the author."
  • **Spanish:** "El libro fue escrito por el autor."

📝 How to Form It:

Use **ser + past participle** (agreeing in gender and number).

  • **El libro fue escrito.** (The book was written.)
  • **Las cartas fueron enviadas.** (The letters were sent.)
